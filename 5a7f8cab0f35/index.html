The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Sky Lakes Medical Center is running Epic, Sectra PACS side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Sky Lakes Medical Center already runs No named third-party engine to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. A November 2020 ransomware attack took down Epic, PACS, Vocera, ~2,500 PCs and ~600 servers simultaneously — staff reverted to paper charting and fax machines for days, showing how tightly coupled/interdependent the clinical systems were with no resilient, isolated interface layer.

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
