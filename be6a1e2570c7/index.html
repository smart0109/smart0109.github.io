The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Phillips County Health Systems is running Likely Oracle Health, Persona Pay side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Zero IT/informatics/interface job postings found anywhere for this facility — suggests a very small or nonexistent in-house IT/integration team, fully dependent on Cerner's hosted/managed services rather than owning any integration layer. The listed IT Director appears to have left the role (now runs an unrelated business) — a possible IT leadership gap — with concurrent lab-staffing openings.

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
