The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Hocking Valley Community Hospital is running Likely athenahealth, Siemens MAGNETOM Aera 1.5T MRI via 2022 Captive Radiology partnership side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Hocking Valley Community Hospital already runs Likely InterSystems Ensemble + Epic Bridges at the OSU Wexner Medical Center shared-IT-services layer to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. A 2024 financial audit shows HVCH's net position collapsed from $4.8M to ~$240K in one year (days-cash-on-hand fell from 63 to 21) with auditors raising 'going concern' doubts — a hospital this financially strained almost certainly has an under-resourced IT/informatics team unable to invest in integration tooling on its own. IT Director Chad Moore was recently promoted to CIO, citing rising IT/cybersecurity complexity even at this small critical-access hospital.

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
