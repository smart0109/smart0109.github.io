The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

San Joaquin Valley Rehabilitation Hospital is running Likely Cerner or a rehab EHR, Outsourced mobile/portable imaging likely, Likely Inovalon EASE All-Payer Solution — parent Vibra Healthcare + Ernest Health selected it systemwide side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, San Joaquin Valley Rehabilitation Hospital already runs Vibra Healthcare is listed in Redox Engine's connected-organization directory to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. California's AB 133 Data Exchange Framework legally mandates electronic data exchange between acute hospitals, rehab/LTACs, health plans, and labs; the rehab/LTAC compliance deadline (Jan 31, 2026) has already passed as of today — meaning this hospital is now under an active legal mandate to electronically exchange referral/transfer data with its feeder acute hospitals (Community Regional Medical Center, Saint Agnes, Clovis Community, Kaiser Fresno), a concrete, dated compliance hook. The Inovalon EASE 5-year revenue-cycle contract (Nov 2020) is likely up for renewal/renegotiation around late 2025 — a relevant outreach-timing signal.

Even where the stack looks tidy on paper, BridgeGate's job is making sure every one of these systems agrees on the same patient record.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
