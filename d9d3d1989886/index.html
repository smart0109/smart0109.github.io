The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Roseland Hospital is running Not publicly confirmed, eRAD cloud PACS, In-house pharmacy department confirmed side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Roseland Hospital already runs No third-party engine to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. A 2025 data breach (names, DOB, medical record numbers, insurance/diagnosis data exposed) plus an ongoing 2025-2026 financial emergency causing payroll shortfalls — both point to an under-resourced, legacy, high-risk IT environment reliant on older point-to-point HL7 interfaces rather than a modern integration layer.

Even where the stack looks tidy on paper, BridgeGate's job is making sure every one of these systems agrees on the same patient record.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
