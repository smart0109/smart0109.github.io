The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Lincoln Community Hospital and Care Center is running Likely Oracle Health, Radiology reads outsourced to RIA, Reference/send-out lab work routed to Quest or Labcorp, TruBridge side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Parent Community Hospital Corporation (CHC) created 'Options RCM' specifically to help small/rural CHC-managed hospitals like Lincoln 'improve financial health' — a signal these facilities generally lack modern, integrated revenue-cycle tooling and rely on CHC's shared-services model rather than in-house systems. Cerner EHR (via IQHealth) paired with a TruBridge-run billing operation is a fragmented, two-vendor stack; outsourced radiology (RIA) and reference labs (Quest/Labcorp) both require electronic result/image integration maintained by effectively one combined Lab/Clinical-Informatics role.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
