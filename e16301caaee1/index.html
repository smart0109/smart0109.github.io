The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Granite Hills Hospital's core system is Likely Oracle Health (Cerner) — UHS enterprise behavioral EHR — even a single-EHR shop like this one still has to reconcile lab, imaging, pharmacy, and payer data that live outside the EHR proper. On top of that, Granite Hills Hospital already runs Same UHS/Crossings Healthcare Solutions Cerner Millennium customization ecosystem applies corporately to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. No public referral/transfer technology connects Granite Hills to Milwaukee-area acute-care systems (Froedtert, Aurora/Advocate) — its contact page directs providers to call for referrals with no portal or electronic hand-off described. A 2022-2023 Wisconsin DQA inspection substantiated medication-security failures and monitoring/documentation gaps amid severe staffing shortages, during a slow bed-capacity ramp (48 of a planned 120 beds as of 2024) — concrete compliance/operational strain, though no specific system was named as the cause.

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
