The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Southern Coos Hospital & Health Center is running Epic, GE Revolution EVO 64-slice CT scanner side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Historical records from before the Dec 2024 go-live stayed trapped in the legacy MyCareCorner portal, not scheduled to merge into MyChart until 'early 2025' — a real post-migration data-continuity gap.

Your hospital is mid-transition right now — the highest-leverage moment to bring BridgeGate in before the new interfaces calcify.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
