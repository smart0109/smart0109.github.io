The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Newman Memorial Hospital is running Likely Epic, Billing split across two units: hospital Business Office side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Newman relies on SSM Health's own MyChart infrastructure (mychart.ssmhc.com) rather than a locally-hosted portal, implying Newman's ambulatory/patient data already flows into a larger SSM Health data environment — at least one existing external interface relationship worth probing in discovery. Medical-records requests are handled entirely manually (fax/mail/in-person, fee schedule still dated 2015) — no ROI/HIM automation vendor at all. Ruled out as a recent Oracle Health CommunityWorks adopter (not in the 2023/2024 press releases naming new customers).

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
