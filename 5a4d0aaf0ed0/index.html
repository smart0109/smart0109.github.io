The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Mason District Hospital is running Likely TruBridge/CPSI, GE Discovery PET/CT mobile gantry, TruBridge side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Mason District Hospital already runs HL7 confirmed as the mechanism returning teleradiology reports from OnRad/Carestream back to the hospital to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. Uses OnRad Inc. as an outsourced 24/7 teleradiology reading partner — images and reports must flow off-site to an external radiology group with no publicly documented integration engine tying that hand-off together.

Even where the stack looks tidy on paper, BridgeGate's job is making sure every one of these systems agrees on the same patient record.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
