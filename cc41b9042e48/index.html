The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Houston Behavioral Healthcare Hospital's core system is Not publicly disclosed (Signature Healthcare Services) — even a single-EHR shop like this one still has to reconcile lab, imaging, pharmacy, and payer data that live outside the EHR proper. Referral process is explicitly manual — a 24-hour phone intake line plus a dedicated fax line, with providers asked to 'call to verify your fax was received' — a textbook fax-based behavioral-to-acute hand-off gap with zero electronic connectivity. No CIO/IT/HIM-titled leader appears on the hospital's published executive roster — consistent with IT being centrally managed by parent Signature Healthcare Services rather than locally. As a freestanding psychiatric hospital it's excluded from CMS Promoting Interoperability attestation data.

With a lean IT team stretched thin, the pitch isn't 'do more work' — it's 'let BridgeGate and AI carry the integration load so your team doesn't have to.'

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
