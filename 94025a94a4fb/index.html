The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Beloit Health System is running Likely Oracle Health, Likely Merge Healthcare, Likely CareFusion/Pyxis, Likely nThrive revenue-cycle software — inferred from recurring donor listings 2018-2023 side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Beloit Health System already runs Live participant in WISHIN to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. A planned joint micro-hospital venture with FHN (Freeport, IL) fell through, and FHN is now merging into Mercyhealth — which also runs a competing 'Mercyhealth Beloit' location in the same town. A fluid, fragmented regional hospital landscape with shifting partners/systems nearby. IT Director Adam Desaulniers (titled 'Information Systems/Cerner' then 'Information Systems/Oracle') departed for Vail Health after the 2023 annual report — a recent IT leadership gap.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
