The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Desert Valley Hospital & Medical Group is running Likely Epic, Likely Agfa DX-D 100 mobile DR units, Likely Omnicell — Prime Healthcare's systemwide partnership since 2017 covers 'a majority of facilities', Epic revenue-cycle module confirmed as part of the systemwide Epic conversion side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Prime Healthcare is mid-migration from a historically mixed MEDITECH/varied-EHR environment across its 45+ hospital system to a single Epic instance — this scale of standardization typically requires significant interface remapping (ADT, lab, imaging, pharmacy feeds) system-wide. A Prime Healthcare IT executive confirmed (2018) the system ran Epic AND Meditech concurrently pre-conversion; Desert Valley was notably absent from Prime's initial 2019 Manifest MedEx (CA HIE) cohort of 8 hospitals, suggesting it may lag peer facilities on HIE participation.

Your hospital is mid-transition right now — the highest-leverage moment to bring BridgeGate in before the new interfaces calcify.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
