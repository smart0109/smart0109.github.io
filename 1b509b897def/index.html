The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Opelousas General Health System is running Likely Oracle Health, Stratus PACS — confirmed, Likely PioneerRx side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Opelousas General Health System already runs Historical: Orion Health HIE platform to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. OGHS engaged an outside advisory firm for a multi-year, ground-up IT overhaul spanning EMR/imaging/network/security — and needed to fully reconfigure its existing PACS just for basic speed/stability — both point to a fragmented, underperforming legacy stack that a middleware/integration engine could target directly. CEO confirms ~$15-16M/year IT spend; an outdated PACS needing an $800K-1M independent purchase was instead resolved via the Triad Executive Advisors shared-hosting/MSP model, which is now expanding to a 4th partner health system — an active, CEO-championed vendor-consolidation trend.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
