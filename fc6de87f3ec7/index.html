Why BridgeGate, why now

Summit Oaks Hospital's core system is Likely Oracle Health (Cerner) — UHS enterprise behavioral EHR — even a single-EHR shop like this one still has to reconcile lab, imaging, pharmacy, and payer data that live outside the EHR proper. Admissions route through a phone-based 'Intake Center' with zero public mention of EHR interoperability, HIE, or CommonWell/Carequality — combined with UHS's slow, multi-year facility-by-facility Cerner rollout, real risk Summit Oaks is either still on a legacy/paper-adjacent system or a newly-live Cerner instance not yet interfaced to referring NJ acute-hospital EDs. NJ DOH issued (then rescinded) a 2024 Directed Plan of Correction over violence-prevention and documentation deficiencies from a 2023 survey — recent regulatory/compliance distress, not IT-specific but relevant outreach-timing context.

Even where the stack looks tidy on paper, BridgeGate's job is making sure every one of these systems agrees on the same patient record.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
