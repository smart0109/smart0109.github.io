The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Titusville Area Hospital is running Shares Meadville Medical Center’s EHR platform, Likely Viztek Opal-RAD PACS, RxLocal side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. UPMC operates its own outpatient center and primary-care practice directly in Meadville (separate from MMC), meaning patients in the same small town likely move between two unaffiliated records environments (MMC's system and UPMC's Epic) — a classic fragmented-records scenario.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
