The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Indianapolis Rehabilitation Hospital is running Likely a rehab-specialty EHR, WellSky Interactant native Pharmacy Information System module, WellSky Interactant native RCM/GL module side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Indianapolis Rehabilitation Hospital already runs None named as a third party to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. Parent Nobis's own announcement cites eliminating 'manual documentation scanning' for lab/radiology/transcription data as a driver for the EHR switch — referral/ancillary handoffs were largely paper/fax-based; no evidence the hospital connects to Indiana's statewide HIE (IHIE) despite receiving transfers from Ascension St. Vincent, IU Health, and Community Health Network.

Your hospital is mid-transition right now — the highest-leverage moment to bring BridgeGate in before the new interfaces calcify.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
