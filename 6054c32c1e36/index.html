The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Nashville General Hospital is running Oracle Health, Sectra RIS/PACS — confirmed deployed contract, In-house 'Nashville Healthcare Pharmacy' — no software vendor named side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. CIO Melanie Thomas said on record: 'We are replacing several disparate systems that required redundant data entry, disrupted our operations during upgrades, and lacked integration between our inpatient and ambulatory systems' — the clearest quotable pain-point statement found in this entire hospital set, with a stated goal of 'one patient, one record.'

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
