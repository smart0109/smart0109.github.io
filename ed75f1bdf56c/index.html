The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Fairmount Behavioral Health System is running Likely Oracle Health, Confirmed in-house pharmacy department, Cerner Millennium Revenue Cycle side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Fairmount Behavioral Health System already runs No third-party engine; UHS's in-house subsidiary 'Crossings Healthcare Solutions' builds/customizes Cerner Millennium modules for the behavioral rollout to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. UHS's multi-year, facility-by-facility Oracle Health rollout across 200+ behavioral sites means Fairmount is likely mid-migration with inconsistent EHR versions/configurations, while its referral process (24-hour phone line, contact form) shows no electronic referral connectivity to referring acute-care EDs. UHS-wide, the Sept 2020 Ryuk ransomware attack took EHR systems offline at ALL US facilities, forcing paper charting and costing UHS a disclosed $67M — evidence of a centrally-managed UHS network with a documented full-outage history.

Your hospital is mid-transition right now — the highest-leverage moment to bring BridgeGate in before the new interfaces calcify.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
