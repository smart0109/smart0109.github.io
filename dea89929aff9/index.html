The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

AdCare Hospital is running Likely CPSI/Evident 'Thrive', Likely TruBridge revenue-cycle bundle side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Parent AAC leadership has publicly stated it is 'transitioning toward a unified electronic medical record system' and consolidating 'disparate systems' across its 30+ facility network — implying AdCare runs a legacy/siloed system today. Compounding this, AAC and 8 affiliated facilities (including Massachusetts) suffered a Sept 2024 ransomware attack exposing ~410,747 patients' data, settled for $2.75M in 2025 — the strongest multi-facility fragmentation + security pain signal found across all 44 hospitals. AAC Holdings' own 10-K risk factors explicitly flag dependency on IT/claims systems as a named corporate risk factor.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
