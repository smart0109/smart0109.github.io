The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Memorial Community Hospital & Health System is running Likely Epic, RevSpring PersonaPay side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. Your hospital shows up in both a CHI Health-branded MyChart portal AND a Methodist Health System (Omaha) locations directory — if both relationships are real, the hospital may need to interface with more than one larger health system's data environment simultaneously. Current IT job posting describes a lean generalist team (M365/AD/LAN, no dedicated clinical-systems/informatics role) — likely leans on CHI Health/CommonSpirit for EHR/PACS/LIS support; parent CommonSpirit is mid-rollout on an Epic 'OneEHR' consolidation (65% of hospitals converted as of 2026, targeting 2030), a plausible future path for this facility.

Sound familiar? That's the exact situation on the ground here.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
