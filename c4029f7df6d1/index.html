The Integration Pitch

Why BridgeGate, why now

Mid-Valley Hospital & Clinic is running Oracle Health, Redde Payments side by side. Every one of those speaks a different dialect of HL7/FHIR/X12 — which means someone on staff is today doing by hand (or not doing at all) what BridgeGate does automatically: keeping one patient's record consistent everywhere it needs to show up. On top of that, Mid-Valley Hospital & Clinic already runs Cerner HealtheIntent to try to bridge some of these systems — a sign the integration burden is already visible to their own team, not just to us. Public materials are largely silent on backend IT/interoperability infrastructure — for a small critical-access hospital this typically means the Cerner CommunityWorks bundled model is used largely as-is, a good discovery-call question rather than a documented pain point. 2024 DOJ enforcement action ($15,000 penalty) over a nurse's opioid diversion — a concrete medication-dispensing/diversion-monitoring control gap.

Even where the stack looks tidy on paper, BridgeGate's job is making sure every one of these systems agrees on the same patient record.

The payoff is concrete: one integration layer retires the redundant interfaces and manual data movement that quietly drain the budget, streamlining operations and taking roughly 35% out of what a hospital your size spends on IT.
